The Research Plan

Brief Summary of Insights Gained to Date

  • Summarize key takeaways from customer interviews, stakeholder discussions, and any early research.
  • Identify trends, pain points, or needs that have emerged.
  • Highlight what has worked well in your approach and what hasn’t.
  • Mention what adjustments you plan to make going forward (e.g., refining questions, targeting different respondents, adjusting methodology).

From the customer interviews, I noticed the following important insights:

  1. People are not comfortable with wearing extra glasses to track their eye movement, especially when they already wear glasses.
  2. Following where attendees look in radiology images can have educational purposes, potentially helping residents learn how and where to focus on different images.
  3. The main concern is finding all the important information in the images and reporting it.
  4. The main pain point is avoiding missed abnormalities and misdiagnoses.

This stage has resulted in changing the topic to another idea (Imagulator) and proceeding with that topic.

Target Subjects

My target subjects are physicians, including radiologists, neurologists, oncologists, orthopedic surgeons, and cardiologists. These are my end-users.

I’m reaching out to them by email and setting up online meetings to ask questions. I may also distribute surveys for participants I don’t know directly.

Research Objectives

  • Determine the primary reasons clinicians would adopt or reject this new tool.
  • Understand clinician concerns about accuracy in detecting image changes.

Methodology

I will conduct interviews via Zoom, phone calls, or in-person meetings — whichever is best for the participant.

I will explore these key questions:

  • How critical or important is seeing different changes in longitudinal images?
  • What would be the best way to visualize these differences?
  • How are you doing it currently?
  • Do you think an assistant like Imagulator would be beneficial? Would you use it?
  • What is the process of adopting new software in your department?

The current sample size is 4 physicians: 2 neurologists and 2 radiologists.
